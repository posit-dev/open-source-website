# Contributing to the Posit open source website

First off, thank you for wanting to contribute. The goal of this site is to celebrate and support the open-source data science ecosystem. We love community additions like blog posts, cheatsheets, and other resources.

Because the Posit Open Source website team is small and maintains this site alongside our daily engineering work, we have a structured process to ensure high-quality content without burning out our maintainers.

Please take a moment to review these guidelines before you start working on a contribution.

Note: These guidelines are for external contributors. Internal blog post contributions from Posit affiliates should follow the guidelines in `content/blog/_authoring-guide.md`.

---

## The golden rule: issue-first workflow

With the exception of minor typos or broken links, **all contributions must begin with an Issue, not a Pull Request.**

1. **Open an Issue:** Before writing code, creating a cheat sheet, or drafting a blog post, open an issue on GitHub describing what you want to add or change.
2. **Get the Green Light:** Wait for a Posit maintainer to review your proposal and reply with an explicit approval (or label the issue as `approved` / `help wanted`).
3. **Submit your PR:** Once approved, reference that issue number in your Pull Request.

*Note: Unsolicited PRs for new features, blogs, or resources that do not reference an approved issue will be closed without review.*

---

## What we are looking for

To keep the site focused and maintainable, we look for content that aligns with our core mission:

### We gladly accept:

* **Package Cheat Sheets:** Up-to-date cheat sheets for core Posit-maintained open-source packages (tidyverse, Quarto, shiny, vetiver, etc.).
* **Technical Blog Posts:** Case studies, unique workflows, or deep-dives highlighting open-source data science tools in action.
* **Typo Fixes & Enhancements:** Minor text corrections, broken link fixes, or CSS adjustments. (No pre-approval issue needed for these.)

### We do not accept:

* **Commercial Promotion:** Content focused primarily on promoting a paid, third-party tool or service.
* **Duplicate Content:** Cheat sheets or blogs that cover topics we already have extensively detailed on the site.
* **Low-Effort / AI-Generated Bulk Content:** Submissions that appear to be generated by LLMs without technical depth or unique insight.

---

## Content guidelines

### For blog posts

* Must be written in Markdown or Quarto (`.qmd`).
* All code chunks (R, Python, etc.) must render successfully and cleanly.
* Imagery/data used must be publicly available and properly credited.

### For cheat sheets & resources

* PDF/PNG uploads must be high-resolution and accessible.
* Must utilize clear, legible fonts and adhere loosely to Posit's brand style (clean, data-centric).

---

## Step-by-step contribution process

If your issue is approved:

1. **Fork the repo** and create your branch from `main`.
2. **Build locally** to ensure your changes render correctly and don't introduce build errors.
3. **Keep it focused:** Ensure your PR only addresses the approved issue. Do not bundle multiple unrelated changes together.
4. **Open the PR:** Fill out the PR template completely.

## Review timelines

We are a small team. We review community contributions on a bi-weekly basis. Please expect **up to two weeks** for an initial review. We appreciate your patience and your contribution to the community.
