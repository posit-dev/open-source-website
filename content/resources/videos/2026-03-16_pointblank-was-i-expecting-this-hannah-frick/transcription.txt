<h3>What pointblank gives you</h3>

<p><a data-t="70">The main thing you get from pointblank is a beautiful HTML table like this.</a> <a data-t="76">You can stick it in a report, or you can have it emailed to you.</a> <a data-t="76">And roughly one row is one validation role.</a> <a data-t="82">The specifications are to the left, and the results are to the right.</a> <a data-t="88">So, if you're validating the contents of a particular column, that's the rows, those are the units.</a> <a data-t="93">It will tell you how many and how many passed and how many failed.</a> <a data-t="93">And life is messy, and your data might be too.</a> <a data-t="101">But in both cases, you can typically tolerate some amount of that.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="101">So pointblank lets you specify thresholds for when you want to warn, stop, or be notified.</a> <a data-t="110">And if you specify that, at minimum, it drives the colors to the left of that table.</a> <a data-t="116">But really, it can do a lot more, because it can trigger any action that you can express in R code.</a> <a data-t="127">And lastly, that column to the right is for when things go wrong.</a> <a data-t="127">You can get access to a CSV with the units or rows that failed that condition.</a></p>
