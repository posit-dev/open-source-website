  This week''s guest was Chris Gates, Director of the Bioinformatics Core at University of Michigan!


  Some topics covered in this week''s Hangout were how bioinformatics is at the intersection of molecular biology, statistics, and computer science; balancing continued education across multiple complex fields (wow, does this sound hard); AI''s role in bioinformatics and computational reproducibility; and career paths as a specialist versus generalist. What do you think? Are you Team Specialist or Team Generalist? 🤔


  One community member asked: "Since bioinformatics is the intersection of three highly complex fields, how do you balance continued education and professional development with all three?"


  Chris''s paraphrased answer: The three domains (molecular biology, statistics, and computer science) are not static, regular shapes, they''re constantly shifting and evolving. There are regular shocks to the system, like when the bioinformatics community had to transition from Snakemake to Nextflow because the developer community eclipsed the offerings. It''s a dynamic balance you have to continuously attend to. If you want to master something static, study Ancient Latin or Greek. In bioinformatics, you''re always one meeting away from looking ignorant about something, but that''s the trade-off. Do you want to look smart, or do you want to learn something? The diversity of projects constantly tests where you should go next, and every consultation is an opportunity to learn.
