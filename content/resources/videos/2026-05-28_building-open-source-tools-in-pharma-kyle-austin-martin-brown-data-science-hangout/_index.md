  This week''s guests were Kyle Austin, Principal Programmer Analyst at PPD, and Martin Brown, Statistical Science Director at PPD (the Contract Research side of Thermo Fisher Scientific)!


  Some topics covered in this week''s Hangout were clinical research and contract research organizations (CROs), transitioning from SAS to R (and open source generally) in the pharmaceutical industry, data quality and governance in clinical trials, and tool development with multi-language programming.


  One community member asked: "How do you make the case that moving to open source is positive if the outputs are similar to closed source?"


  Martin Brown''s paraphrased answer: Having more tools is important - SAS has its strengths, but there will be things that newer coding and open source will do better and be more flexible about. As a CRO taking on different types of projects that get wider and more diverse, being multilingual is really important. The variety of ways we''re digesting data is getting bigger, so we need different tools to be able to pivot and understand data in different situations.
