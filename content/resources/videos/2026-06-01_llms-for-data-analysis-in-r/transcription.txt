<h3>How LLMs work</h3>

<p><a data-t="1891">We've talked a bit about how conversations work with LLMs, statelessness, and sending</a> <a data-t="1899">this full history. So, we're going to talk really briefly at a high level about how they actually</a> <a data-t="1903">work, how they understand what you're saying, and how they construct responses.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1911">So, LLMs, you might know, are trained on this huge corporate data, essentially everything that's</a> <a data-t="1915">been written on the internet, books they have access to, all of this text and information.</a> <a data-t="1922">They extract the patterns from that and through that develop the ability to simulate,</a> <a data-t="1927">understand a context, generating text that matches your request. And they kind of understand how</a> <a data-t="1933">language works. So, they can do things like answer your questions, write code, and translate.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1939">But LLMs don't generate whole sentences at once, and they also don't generate necessarily</a> <a data-t="1944">whole words at once. Instead, they work token by token. So, they think in tokens, which are these</a> <a data-t="1951">fundamental units of LLMs. You can kind of think of a token like a word, but it is not necessarily</a> <a data-t="1959">a word. So, for example, hello is one token, but the word unconventional, which is a bit longer,</a> <a data-t="1966">the word unconventional, which is a bit longer, is split into three tokens.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1971">And it's important to know about tokens because this defines their input and output limits</a> <a data-t="1977">for individual requests, as well as API pricing, which is usually broken down by millions of</a> <a data-t="1983">tokens. And tokens are just for words. So, images can also be tokenized as well.</a></p>

<h3>How to think about working with LLMs</h3>

<p><a data-t="2156">So, we talked about, at a high level, how LLMs work, how they generate tokens, that they are stateless.</a> <a data-t="2190">And I just want to talk really briefly about how to think about working with LLMs.</a></p>
