<h3>Small-format community events</h3>

<p><a data-t="71">So idea number one, how about a coffee break?</a> <a data-t="79">This is 45 minutes.</a> <a data-t="79">We do this monthly.</a> <a data-t="79">Bring whatever beverage you have to have.</a> <a data-t="84">Join when you can.</a> <a data-t="84">Leave when you have to.</a> <a data-t="84">So this is a nice place to chat in a relatively small group.</a> <a data-t="89">You can do things like meet people's pets or see their apartments.</a> <a data-t="94">You can hear about their latest art project or talk about the weather.</a> <a data-t="101">And sometimes this is also the right environment for more deep and involved conversations like how are you really doing?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="108">This has landed really well with people.</a> <a data-t="108">And yet what else can we do?</a> <a data-t="108">Who could we reach by doing something slightly different?</a> <a data-t="116">Enter the pride game break.</a> <a data-t="116">This is half an hour every month.</a> <a data-t="121">We get folks together and do something fun, play some kind of game together.</a> <a data-t="121">So we've had Jeopardy, trivia games, drawing games like Gartic Phone.</a> <a data-t="127">I think this works really well because it gives people a third thing, something to do together, something to connect through without the same dynamics as a direct full-on conversation.</a></p>

<h3>Queer lightning talks</h3>

<p><a data-t="141">Okay, that's great.</a> <a data-t="141">What else?</a> <a data-t="141">The newest addition to our lineup is something we're calling Queer Lightning Talks.</a> <a data-t="146">So yes, you have the dynamic I described earlier of people all paying attention to the same person, but the presentations are short</a> <a data-t="151">and the people speaking are our own.</a> <a data-t="157">Topics are completely open-ended, work, non-work, whatever folks choose to speak about.</a> <a data-t="163">Some recent talk topics include a deep dive on animal communication</a> <a data-t="168">from someone with a PhD in neuroscience, a story complete with visuals about the challenging journey of knitting your first pair of socks,</a> <a data-t="174">a run-through of a previous iteration of this talk,</a> <a data-t="179">and a five-minute walkthrough of how to turn yourself from a human into a creature from another planet.</a></p>
